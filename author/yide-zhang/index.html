Yide Zhang

Incoming Assistant Professor of ECEE and BME

University of Colorado Boulder

My name is Yide Zhang (Chinese: 张以德). I specialize in advancing optical imaging technologies, focusing on improving their speed, accuracy, and accessibility for translational research. With a foundation in electrical engineering, particularly in biomedical imaging and optics, my PhD work at the University of Notre Dame involved developing enhancements for multiphoton fluorescence lifetime imaging microscopy and super-resolution microscopy. These innovations reduced the time and cost of image generation substantially. As a postdoc at Caltech, my research has broadened to include the development of cutting-edge photoacoustic imaging, quantum imaging, and ultrafast imaging techniques. These advancements are pivotal for capturing and studying ultrafast biophysical processes that were previously unobservable, impacting our understanding of complex biological systems and enhancing the capabilities of medical diagnostics. In my free time, I enjoy cooking, hiking, cycling, and traveling.

I will join the University of Colorado Boulder as an Assistant Professor of Electrical, Computer & Energy Engineering and Biomedical Engineering in May 2025. My lab will be actively recruiting postdocs, graduate students, and CU Boulder undergraduates. If you are interested, please send your CV to yide.zhang@colorado.edu.
